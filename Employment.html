Help desk technician typically responding to customer inquiries about technical issues, troubleshooting problems with computer hardware and software, guiding users through step-by-step solutions over the phone or remotely, and providing basic training on new technology, essentially acting as the first point of contact for technical support within a company or organization. Example scenarios of what a help desk technician might do:

  • Answering a call about a user's computer not connecting to the internet: Diagnosing the problem by asking questions about network settings, troubleshooting basic connection issues, and guiding the user through steps to resolve the issue.
  • Resolving a password reset request: Verifying the user's identity and guiding them through the password reset process on the company's system.
  • Troubleshooting printer issues: Assisting a user with troubleshooting a printer that isn't printing properly, including checking ink levels, paper jams, and connection settings.
  • Installing new software on a user's computer: Remotely guiding a user through the installation process of a new software application.
  • Providing basic training on a new company software: Walking new employees through the features and functionality of a new software program.
  • Logging and tracking support tickets: Recording details of each technical issue reported by users in a ticketing system to monitor progress and follow up on resolutions.

Key skills as Help Desk Technician:

  • Excellent communication skills
  • Strong problem-solving abilities
  • Technical knowledge of computer hardware and software
  • Customer service orientation
  • Ability to explain complex technical issues in simple terms
